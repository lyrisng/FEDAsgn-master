Summary for USA

The U.S. has nearly a quater of the world's cases and a fifth of all deaths. There are more Americans that died from Covid-19 than in World War II. As Covid-19 became the third leading cause of death in the U.S. in 2020, behind heart disease and cancer. President Donald Trump declard the U.S. outbreak a public health emergency when the first american case was reported though the initial U.S. response to the pandemic was slow in terms of preparing the healthcare system, stopping other travels and etc. Not only that, Trump downplayed the threat posed by the virus and claimed that it was under control when it is clearly not. The situation got serious when the first known American deaths occurred in Feburary and Trump then signed the Coronavirus Preparedness and Response Supplemental Appropriations Act, which provided $8.3 billion in emergency funding for federal agencies to respond to the outbreak. (Wikipedia.org)

Summary for India

India currently holds the 2nd highest number of confirmed cases in the world after USA with more than 10 million reported Covid-19 Cases. The cases peaked during mid-September 2020 when the daily reports went over 90,000 cases. On July 2020, India's Ministry of Information and Broadcasting claimed the country's case fatality rate was among the lowest at 2.41% and "steadily declining". Fast forward to 10 june, India's recoveries exceeded active cases for the first time as infection rates started to drop significantly in September, and the number of daily new cases and active cases started to decline rapidly and may come under control by Feburary 2021. (Wikipedia.org)

Summary for Brazil

The first case in brazil happened on 25 Feburary 2020 when a man from Sao Paulo tested positive for the virus. The diesease had spread to every state in Brazil by 21 March which led to the country reporting its 1 millionth case on 19 June 2020. Brazil currently holds the third-highest number of confirmed Covid-19 cases in the world behind India and on 8 Auguest 2020, the country hit its 3 millionth case. The pandemic triggered a variety of responses from the federal, state and local governments, having an impact on politics, education, the environment, and the economy. On 27 March, Brazil announced a temporary ban on foreign air travelers and most state governors have imposed quarantines to prevent the spread of the virus. (Wikipedia.org)

Summary for Russia

Russia confirmed that the virus have reached the country when two Chinese citizens have been tested positive for Covid-19 on 31 Janurary 2020. Russia took early prevention measures including restricting the border with china and extensive testing but the infection started to spread from Italy from 2 March leading to additional measures taken such as cancelling events, closing schools, theatres and museums as well as shutting the border and declaring a non-working period which lasted for two extensions. Currently, Russia has the fourth-highest number of confirmed cases in the world and first in Europe. (Wikipedia.org)

Summary for United Kingdom

United Kingdom has the world's fourth-highest death rate per hundred thousand population and there are close to 4,000,000 confirmed cases as of 29 Janurary 2021 making it the fifth highest number of confirmed cases in the world. There has been some disparity between the outbreak's severity in each of the four nations. Health in the United kingdom is a devolved matter with England, Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales having their own systems of publicly funded healthcare, seperate governments and parliaments, together with a smaller private sector and voluntary provision. In March 2020, the UK governments imposed a stay-at-home order, dubbed "Stay Home, Protect the NHS, Save Lives", banning all non-essential travel and closing most gathering places. People were told to keep apart in public. Police were enforced stricter rules and the Coronavirus Act 2020 gave all four governments emergency powers which was not used since the Second World War. In December, the UK became the first country to authorise and begin the use of Pfizer/BioNTech Covid-19 Vaccine in a mass vaccination programme throughout 2021. (Wikipedia.org)
